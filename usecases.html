Use Cases: Pilot Cities

The SmartUrbanity project implements and tests its solutions in five diverse urban contexts, each facing unique challenges in accessibility, urban mobility, and social inclusion.

Rome, Italy

Focus: Improving mixed urban mobility in peripheral neighborhoods. The pilot involves real-time mobility monitoring and strategies to enhance the 15-minute accessibility model in a complex, historic city layout.

Karlsruhe, Germany

Focus: Enhancing integration between public transport and active/shared mobility modes. The pilot tests multimodal hubs and participatory planning methods in mid-sized urban areas.

İzmir, Türkiye

Focus: Addressing challenges in multimodal transport in rapidly growing urban districts. Pilot activities promote inclusive mobility access, especially for women and low-income communities.

Lyon Metropolitan Area, France

Focus: Promoting last-mile connectivity and safety in peri-urban zones. The pilot uses gamification and citizen data to co-design solutions for equitable access to daily services.

Thurgau, Switzerland

Focus: Coordinating mobility services in rural and suburban settings. The pilot integrates demand-responsive transport and evaluates accessibility gaps through spatial analysis.
