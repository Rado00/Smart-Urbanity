About the Project

SmartUrbanity is a European innovation project funded under the DUT (Driving Urban Transitions) partnership. It seeks to design and test digital, participatory, and inclusive solutions for implementing the 15-minute city concept in diverse urban environments.

Project Vision

The project envisions cities where all essential services — such as work, education, health, retail, and recreation — are accessible within a 15-minute walk or bike ride. SmartUrbanity promotes accessibility, proximity, and livability through a people-centered approach powered by technology and collaboration.

Objectives

  • Design citizen-oriented digital tools to assess local accessibility and mobility needs
  • Co-create solutions through participatory engagement at neighborhood level
  • Implement use cases across five European cities with diverse mobility and governance contexts
  • Provide actionable policy support and guidance to urban planners and decision-makers

Who It’s For

The project benefits a wide range of stakeholders — from city authorities and mobility providers to citizens and vulnerable populations — by improving access to jobs, services, and urban opportunities.
